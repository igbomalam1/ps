Understanding Static vs Dynamic Websites

Static Website:
Imagine you have a paper brochure (like a menu or a flyer) that shows the same information to everyone who reads it. That's like a static website. It's like a digital version of that paper brochure, with fixed content that doesn't change unless someone manually updates it.

  • Like a paper brochure
  • Showcase few content
  • limited interactions
  • Same for everyone
  • Best for describing the center of the project
  • Costs less than $30/year for maintainance

Dynamic Website:
Now, imagine a magical paper that can change its content depending on who's reading it, like a menu that shows different options based on your dietary preferences! That's like a dynamic website. It's like a digital paper that can interact with you, show you different things, and even remember your name.

  • Like a magical paper
  • Content changes based on user interactions
  • Can remember your name and preferences
  • Different for each user
  • More complex to make, like building a robot
  • Costs range from $100/year for maintainance

As someone without coding knowledge, think of it like this:

  • Static websites are like a simple poster with fixed information.
  • Dynamic websites are like an interactive kiosk that can adapt to your needs.

Dynamic websites offer a more engaging experience, but require more expertise to create. While static websites are simpler, they can still be very effective for sharing basic information.
